Jilebi

About the dish

Jilebi is an indian sweet that everyone can enjoy. Jalebi is one of the most popular desserts of India and is a spiralled dish that needs time and practice. This crispy and crunchy dessert is dipped in sugar syrup and is loaded with flavours. As per historical texts, the origin of Jalebi is traced to West Asia and it got its unique name from an Arabic word-zulabiya, also known as the Persian zolbiya. It is often used as an anecdote by calling complex people, 'Jalebi ki tarah tedha' or complicated like a jalebi!

Ingredients

  • 3 cup all purpose flour
  • 2 cup hung curd
  • 1/2 cup ghee
  • 3 cup sugar
  • 5 strand saffron
  • 1/2 teaspoon powdered green cardamom
  • 1/2 cup corn flour
  • 1 1/2 pinch baking soda
  • 2 cup sunflower oil
  • 3 cup water
  • 4 drops rose essence
  • 1/2 teaspoon edible food color

Steps

  1. Prepare the Jalebi batter and let it ferment overnight
    1. Mix together all-purpose flour, cornflour,ghee, food color and baking soda in a bowl.
    2. To make a thick batter add hung curd and water and mix well until it is thick but has a slightly flowing consistency.
    3. Mix well until it is thick but has a slightly flowing consistency.
    4. To make the sugar syrup, heat water in a pan over medium flame and add sugar and mix until fully dissolved.
    5. Simmer the syrup until it attains one string consistency and add saffron, cardamom powder and rose essence.
  2. Deep fry the Jalebis.
  3. Soak the Jalebis in sugar syrup and 2-3 minutes and serve warm with Rabri
  4. To make the jalebi even more delicious, add a little bit of ghee to the batter, this will give your jalebi that perfect aroma.
  5. For the crispy Jalebi, don't forget to ferment the batter overnight.
